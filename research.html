Research

We create photofunctional molecular systems by integrating supramolecular design, photochemistry, and autonomous discovery. Our core interest is emergent function from assemblies, including charge separation, long lived reactive states, and chiral photophysics, with applications to photomedicine, energy conversion, and bioimaging.

Research Overview

Research overview figure

Four integrated research pillars

Supramolecular Photomedicine: Sustained Redox PDT

Supramolecular redox dye assemblies that enable sustained radical generation under low oxygen conditions. We aim to establish a new phototherapy concept by combining controllable self assembly with mechanistic photochemistry.

Emergent Photofunctional Supramolecular Systems

Photofunctions that emerge from complex assemblies in water for small molecule transformation and photocatalysis. We explore multi step energy and electron transfer enabled by mesoscale molecular fields.

Dye Assembly Photophysics for Bioimaging

Accumulation driven photophysics in biological environments. We develop high contrast probes and functional imaging strategies based on controllable dye assemblies.

Autonomous Materials Discovery

AI assisted molecular design and robotic optimization to explore chemical space. We aim to discover unknown functional systems and new governing principles.

Key Concepts

  • Emergent photofunctions from molecular assemblies in water
  • Controllable charge separation and long lived reactive excited states
  • Supramolecular control of redox cycles for sustained photochemistry
  • Chiral photophysics including CD and CPL from non chiral dyes via assembly
  • Autonomous exploration of design rules with AI and robotics
